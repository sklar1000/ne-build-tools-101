## Building Level 3: Partial Solution

Level 3 means you're actually solving part of the customer's problem — not just diagnosing it.

**The pattern**: Take your Level 2 output and make it actionable.

| If your L2 is... | Your L3 could be... |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| A nutrition quiz with a score | A custom meal plan with a shopping list |
| A compliance gap report | A corrected label draft they can send to their printer |
| A JD bias scanner with a score | Rewritten job descriptions ready to post |
| A venue matching quiz | Tours booked at their top 3 venues with a comparison summary |
| A budget calculator | A detailed project plan with timeline and milestones |

**How to build it**: Tell your AI tool:

```
I have a Level 2 assessment tool that [describe what it does].
Now I need to build Level 3 — instead of just scoring/diagnosing,
I want to deliver a [specific deliverable] based on the user's
inputs. The deliverable should be something they can immediately
use — not just read.

Specifically, generate a [plan/report/template/document] that:
- References their specific inputs from the assessment
- Provides actionable steps (not general advice)
- Is downloadable or printable
- Looks professional enough to show their boss/team/partner
```

## Attempting Level 4: Transaction

Level 4 is where entrepreneurship gets real. You're asking a stranger for money or commitment.

### Option A: Payment (Strongest Signal)

1. **Create a Stripe Payment Link**: Go to [dashboard.stripe.com](https://dashboard.stripe.com), create an account, click "Payment Links," create a link with your price and description
2. **Add it to your tool**: Tell AI: "After showing the Level 2 results, add a section that says '[Offer description] for [$amount]. [Guarantee].' Link the button to this Stripe URL: [paste]"
3. **What counts**: Any completed payment = strong signal. Track how many people see the offer vs. how many pay.

### Option B: LOI or Booking (Strong Signal)

1. **Create a Calendly link**: [calendly.com](https://calendly.com) — free for one event type
2. **Add it to your tool**: "After the results, add a section: 'Ready for [next step]? Book a [15/30]-minute [call/pilot/demo].' Link to this Calendly URL: [paste]"
3. **What counts**: Booked = strong signal. No-show = moderate signal (they intended to commit).

### Option C: Deposit or Pre-order (Strongest)

1. **Stripe Payment Link** with a lower amount ($5-$29 refundable deposit)
2. **Frame it**: "Reserve your spot / Lock in early-bird pricing / Join the beta"
3. **What counts**: Money exchanged = the strongest possible signal

## Decision at Sprint End

Before writing your Pretotype Update Memo, make the call:

- **GO if**: You hit your pass threshold. Real strangers engaged. You have evidence.
- **KILL if**: You reached target customers and they explicitly said no, or conversion was far below threshold.
- **ITERATE if**: Mixed signals — some interest but not enough to commit. Change the offer, the price, or the customer segment.
